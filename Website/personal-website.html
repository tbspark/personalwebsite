About Me

Chemical Engineering PhD with Specialization in Pharmaceutical Applications

I am a Chemical Engineering PhD with expertise in computational modeling and hands-on laboratory experience, specializing in granulation, powder handling, and characterization. I have a strong background in pharmaceutical formulation development and process optimization for drug delivery systems.

With four years of proven teaching experience delivering engineering courses at the undergraduate level, I focus on practical applications and student engagement while continuously advancing research in twin-screw granulation technologies.

My work combines rigorous scientific methodology with innovative approaches to solve complex pharmaceutical manufacturing challenges.

Areas of Expertise

Computational Modeling

Advanced expertise in DEM, CFD modeling for pharmaceutical processes. Experience with MATLAB, ANSYS CFD, Star CCM+, and gPROMS for complex simulations of granulation processes.

Pharmaceutical Formulation

Development of controlled-release formulation systems with a focus on powder handling techniques for enhanced API stability during processing.

Twin-Screw Granulation

Pioneer in seeded granulation methodologies with expertise in optimizing screw configurations for improved solid dosage form manufacturing processes.

Process Optimization

Implementation of Quality by Design (QbD) principles to systematically optimize granulation parameters for pharmaceutical manufacturing.

Data Analysis

Skilled in Python/Scikit-learn for quantitative analysis and data-driven process development. Experience with process parameter optimization techniques.

Engineering Education

Teaching experience in CAD, mechanical engineering, MINITAB, and Aspen HYSYS with a strong focus on practical applications and student engagement.
